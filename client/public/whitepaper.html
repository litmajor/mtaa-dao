‚ö†Ô∏è The Problem: Traditional Chamas Are Broken

Traditional community savings groups (chamas) across Africa face critical challenges that limit their effectiveness and put members' funds at risk.

Critical Issues

  • Lack of Transparency (78.5%): Manual record-keeping leads to disputes and trust deficits
  • Single Point of Failure (82.3%): One person controls all funds, risking fraud and mismanagement
  • Slow Processes (71.8%): Monthly meetings cause delayed decisions and missed opportunities
  • Geographic Constraints (65.2%): Physical presence required, excluding diaspora contributions
  • Poor Records (69.7%): Paper ledgers prone to loss and manipulation

üìä Economic Impact

  • KES 5 Billion: Annual fraud losses in Kenya alone
  • KES 3 Billion: Lost diaspora contribution potential
  • 17.5 hours/month: Administrative overhead per chama

‚ö†Ô∏è Risks & Mitigation

We take security and risk management seriously, implementing multiple layers of protection across technical, governance, and operational domains.

Technical Security

  • Smart Contract Audits: 2 independent audits pre-launch, quarterly reviews
  • Bug Bounty Program: Up to 100,000 MTAA rewards
  • Reentrancy Protection: OpenZeppelin ReentrancyGuard implementation
  • Oracle Redundancy: Multiple price sources (Chainlink, Pyth, Uniswap TWAP)
  • Circuit Breakers: Automatic pause on >10% price deviation

Governance Protection

  • Quadratic Voting: Reduces whale dominance
  • Voting Weight Cap: Maximum 10% per address
  • Delegation Limits: 5% max per delegate
  • Reputation Requirements: Minimum thresholds for voting
  • Gas Costs: Economic barrier to Sybil attacks

Phased Deployment

  1. Phase 1: Testnet (3 months) - Extensive testing
  2. Phase 2: Mainnet Beta ($100K TVL cap)
  3. Phase 3: Gradual cap increase based on security audits
  4. Phase 4: Full launch after proven stability
