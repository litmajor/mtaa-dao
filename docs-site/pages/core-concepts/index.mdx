# Core Concepts

Understand the fundamental building blocks of MtaaDAO.

## What You'll Learn

- **DAOs**: Decentralized autonomous organizations and how they work
- **Governance**: How communities make decisions together
- **Treasury**: Managing community funds transparently
- **Voting**: Different voting mechanisms and their applications

## Quick Overview

A **DAO** is a community-owned organization where:
- Members collectively own and govern
- Rules are enforced automatically via smart contracts
- All transactions are transparent and immutable
- No single authority controls decisions

## The MtaaDAO Difference

Traditional organizations have hierarchies and gatekeepers. MtaaDAO removes those barriers:

✓ **Transparent**: Every transaction visible to all members
✓ **Democratic**: One member, one vote (configurable)
✓ **Automated**: Rules execute without intermediaries
✓ **Accountable**: History stored permanently on blockchain
✓ **Accessible**: Works on mobile with low data requirements

## From Mtaa to MetaDAO

MtaaDAO supports different organizational levels:

1. **Mtaa DAO** (Community Level)
   - Neighborhood or group level
   - 10-100+ members
   - Monthly to annual cycles

2. **Collective DAO**
   - Multi-community organization
   - 100-1000+ members
   - Semi-permanent structure

3. **MetaDAO** (Network Level)
   - Federation of DAOs
   - Cross-community coordination
   - Regional or thematic focus
